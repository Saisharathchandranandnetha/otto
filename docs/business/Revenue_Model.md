# Revenue Model: Otto

## 1. Overview
Otto's revenue model is designed to capture value from both the software-as-a-service (SaaS) access and the consumption of AI-driven actions (usage-based). This hybrid model aligns Otto's revenue growth with the customer's operational scaling and reliance on the AI operator.

## 2. Core Revenue Streams

### A. Subscription Revenue (SaaS)
A predictable, recurring revenue stream based on access to the Otto platform and the underlying state machine.
- **Base Platform Access:** Includes the dashboard, approval feed, manual action capabilities, and a baseline number of monthly "Resurrection" document extractions.
- **Tiered Plans:** 
  - *Starter/Free:* Limited to manual approvals and a small document ingestion quota. Allows SMBs to experience the "magic" of onboarding without risk.
  - *Pro:* Unlocks the "Autonomy Ladder", allowing the AI to graduate to autonomous execution. Higher data retention and larger document ingestion limits.

### B. Usage-Based Revenue (Consumption)
Tied directly to the variable costs of running LLMs and the value generated by the agent.
- **Autonomous Action Fees:** Charged per successful autonomous action executed by Otto (e.g., generating and sending a PO, answering a customer support query). This scales linearly with the business's operational volume.
- **Document Ingestion Overage:** Fees for processing documents (invoices, ledgers, WhatsApp chats) beyond the monthly subscription tier allowance.

## 3. Secondary Revenue Opportunities
- **Domain-Specific Playbooks:** Otto's Theme 2 MVP includes hardcoded playbooks for Healthcare, Legal, Manufacturing, etc. Premium, highly specialized playbooks could be sold as add-ons.
- **B2B Payments Integration (Future):** As Otto generates POs and reconciles ledgers, integrating a payment gateway to allow Otto to actually settle invoices could introduce a transaction fee revenue stream (Fintech play).

## 4. Key Metrics to Track
- **Average Revenue Per User (ARPU):** Tracked separately for Subscription vs. Usage.
- **Gross Margin:** Crucial to monitor given the variable API costs (OpenRouter, Twilio). Optimization of LLM usage (e.g., falling back to Gemini 2 when GPT-4o is unnecessary) will drive margin expansion.
- **Net Revenue Retention (NRR):** Driven heavily by the "graduation rate" (when users promote Otto to autonomous, usage-based actions).
