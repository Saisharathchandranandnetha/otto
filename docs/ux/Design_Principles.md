# Design Principles

Otto's design philosophy is built around trust, transparency, and frictionless automation for small business owners.

## 1. Zero-Friction Onboarding ("The Resurrection")
- **Show, Don't Ask**: Eliminate manual data entry. Rely entirely on vision extraction from messy, real-world artifacts (photos, handwritten notes).
- **Time to Value**: The entire process from blank slate to running system must take under 3 minutes.
- **Delightful Transparency**: The "Live Narrated Build" turns a boring loading state into an engaging showcase of the AI's capabilities, building initial confidence.

## 2. The Autonomy Ladder (Earned Trust)
- **Human-Gated by Default**: No impactful action happens without explicit user approval initially.
- **Progressive Delegation**: The system must *ask* for permission to automate only after proving its reliability through manual repetitions (the rule of 3).
- **Safety Nets**: Autonomy is never absolute. Every automated action must be:
  - **Capped**: Spending or volume limits.
  - **Logged**: Clear audit trails.
  - **Reversible**: A 1-hour undo window.
  - **Revocable**: A single, prominent toggle to instantly demote the agent to manual mode.

## 3. Action-Oriented Interface
- **The Feed is the App**: The primary interface is a chronological feed of actions, approvals, and events, rather than static dashboards.
- **Contextual Information**: Every action card must contain all necessary context (draft output, evidence sources, cost-of-delay) to make a decision without navigating away.
- **Cost of Inaction**: Highlight the business impact of pending approvals to drive engagement.

## 4. Professional & Utilitarian Aesthetics
- **Dark Theme + Amber Accent**: Conveys a modern, professional, and slightly industrial tool-like feel.
- **JetBrains Mono**: Reinforces the "operator" and "system log" aesthetic, prioritizing readability for data-heavy views.
