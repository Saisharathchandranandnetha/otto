# User Personas

## 1. Primary Persona: Priya, The Boutique Owner

**Demographics:**
- **Name:** Priya Sharma
- **Age:** 42
- **Location:** Jaipur, India
- **Business:** "Priya's Fashion" - a mid-sized clothing boutique with 3 employees.
- **Tech Fluency:** Medium (comfortable with WhatsApp, Instagram, and basic banking apps, but overwhelmed by complex desktop software like Tally or QuickBooks).

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Wants to expand her business to a second location.
- Needs to know exactly which Kurtis are selling fastest so she can reorder them before the weekend rush.
- Wants to keep track of VIP customers who take items on credit.

**Frustrations:**
- Runs her entire inventory on paper. Frequently loses sales because a size is out of stock and she didn't realize it.
- Has piles of handwritten supplier bills sitting in a shoebox.
- Spends 4 hours every Sunday trying to calculate her monthly spend and chase customer dues via WhatsApp.
- Tried an inventory management app once, but uninstalled it after 10 minutes because typing in her 300+ SKU catalog was impossible.

**Day in the Life (Before Otto):**
Priya opens the store at 10 AM. A customer asks for a Blue Kurti in Size M. Priya spends 5 minutes checking the backroom, only to realize she sold the last one on Tuesday. She makes a mental note to text her supplier, but forgets. At the end of the month, she realizes a major customer hasn't paid their ₹12,000 balance from 3 weeks ago.

**How Otto Helps (The Resurrection):**
Priya takes 15 photos of her most recent supplier bills and her red ledger diary. She uploads them to Otto. In 3 minutes, Otto's Vision Extraction engine reads the handwritten bills, resolves the supplier names, and builds her inventory. Otto immediately notifies her that Blue Kurtis (Size M) are out of stock and drafts a purchase order for her supplier. Priya taps "Approve." Otto sends the PO via WhatsApp.

**How Otto Helps (The Autonomy Ladder):**
After manually approving 3 reorders for Blue Kurtis, Otto offers to take over. Priya sets a ₹10,000 cap. The next time stock runs low, Otto automatically sends the WhatsApp order to the supplier, notifying Priya in the feed. Priya reclaims her Sundays.

## 2. Secondary Persona (Theme 2): Ravi, The Clinic Coordinator

**Demographics:**
- **Name:** Ravi Kumar
- **Age:** 28
- **Location:** Bangalore, India
- **Business:** A busy multi-specialty polyclinic.
- **Tech Fluency:** High (uses scheduling software daily).

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Keep the doctors' calendars fully booked without double-booking.
- Ensure patients receive their lab reports and return for follow-ups.
- Reduce patient wait times in the lobby.

**Frustrations:**
- Constantly alternating between the doctor's calendar, the lab report inbox, and patient WhatsApp messages.
- Misses routine follow-ups because administrative tasks fall through the cracks.
- Cannot automate scheduling because medical advice must be strictly separated from administrative triage.

**How Otto Helps (Domain Engine - Healthcare):**
Ravi enables Otto's Healthcare Playbook. Otto scans the lab inbox and the calendar. It identifies 27 patients who need administrative follow-ups (e.g., "Report is ready, schedule consultation"). Otto drafts 27 personalized WhatsApp messages with scheduling links. Because this is a human-gated workflow, Otto stages the actions in Ravi's feed. Ravi quickly reviews the batch and clicks "Approve." Otto handles the outreach, freeing Ravi to focus on the patients currently in the lobby.

## 3. Secondary Persona (Theme 2): Amit, The HR Manager

**Demographics:**
- **Name:** Amit Patel
- **Age:** 35
- **Location:** Mumbai, India
- **Business:** A growing 50-person logistics startup.
- **Tech Fluency:** High.

**Goals & Motivations:**
- Ensure every new hire has a flawless "Day One" experience.
- Maintain compliance with company policies and payroll documentation.

**Frustrations:**
- Onboarding is highly fragmented. Amit has to email IT for a laptop, message Payroll for bank details, and chase the new hire for policy signatures.
- Delay in one step (IT forgetting the laptop) derails the entire process.
- Constant repetitive coordination tasks drain his ability to focus on strategic hiring.

**How Otto Helps (Domain Engine - HR):**
Amit enables Otto's HR Playbook. When 3 new offers are accepted, Otto automatically detects the signal. It creates a consolidated onboarding checklist. It drafts an IT ticket for laptops, generates the payroll forms, and sends the policy handbook to the new hires. Otto stages this complex, multi-department workflow in Amit's feed. Amit reviews the plan and clicks "Approve." Otto coordinates the handoffs, ensuring nothing is missed for Day One.
